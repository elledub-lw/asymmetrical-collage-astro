A cold email dies the moment it's ignored.

Published work doesn't work that way. The essay you wrote six months ago is still running. Still being read. Still making an argument on your behalf. You are asleep. It is not.

I have been writing on Asymmetrical Collage for four months. 100+ posts. I started sharing them with specific people recently. Not because I needed an audience to write. Because the body of work had become large enough to be evidence.

The shift happens then. Not when you decide to do outreach differently. When the work has accumulated enough to do it without you.

A cold email says: trust me, I'm worth your time. A body of work says: here's the evidence, you decide.

A body of work transfers the burden of proof. It runs the permanent audition without you.
