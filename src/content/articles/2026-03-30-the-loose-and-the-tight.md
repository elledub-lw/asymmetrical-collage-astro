Most of what you call a thought isn't one yet.

It's a held impression. Something that arrived, felt true, and stayed. You never examined it. You just carry it.

We use the same word for both. The loose and the tight. The impression you've been carrying for years, and the idea you've worked through. They feel the same from the inside. They are nothing alike.

For years I held two contradictory truths: I disliked marketing, and I admired Seth Godin. I never resolved it. I just carried both.

Last week I tried to explain it. The contradiction dissolved in the attempt. The word I'd been missing was [generosity](https://www.asymmetricalcollage.com/articles/2026-03-28-not-promotion-generosity/). Not a compromise. A resolution. It was there the whole time. I hadn't thought it through. I'd only held it.

There are many ways from the loose to the tight. Conversation. Debate. Deliberate reflection. Writing is one I practice. Whatever path you use, the move is the same: stop holding it. Work it.
