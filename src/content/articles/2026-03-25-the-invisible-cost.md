The work is done.

The post is written. The site is live. The portfolio page exists. The about page says what it needs to say.

And still — nothing sent. No one told. The world doesn't know yet.

The fear of moving too soon is easy to name. Ship before you're ready and you waste the moment. So you wait. One more refinement. One more post. A little more.

The cost of waiting doesn't announce itself. It just accumulates. Every day you don't ship is a day your work doesn't exist for anyone who values it. The connection you haven't made. That cost is real. It compounds. Nobody tracks it.

Ready is a feeling, not a threshold. The threshold passed weeks ago.
