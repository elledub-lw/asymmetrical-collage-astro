The knowledge economy has two sides. Almost nobody checks which one they're on.

Google knows your political anxieties. Facebook mapped the network of every person you trust. Amazon predicts what you'll buy before you've thought about it. That intelligence didn't come from research. It came from you. Your searches. Your clicks. Your attention, measured in milliseconds, sold in real time.

You were not a customer. You were the product. Not a participant in the knowledge economy. The raw material it ran on.

Most people know this. Fewer have changed anything because of it.

I run my notes on local files. My publishing framework on infrastructure I co-built. My writing tools send nothing to anyone's server unless I say so. Not because privacy is absolute. Because the alternative hands someone else a permanent claim on how I think.

You cannot live on a deserted island. I use Maps. I pay with a card. I publish to the web. Not every tension resolves. The goal is not purity. It is clarity. Know where you made the trade. Know why. Name the exceptions.

The extraction is ongoing either way. The only variable is whether you chose it.
