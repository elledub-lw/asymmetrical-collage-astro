Interest is an appetite. Responsibility is a claim.

I find a lot of things interesting. How team culture shapes collective performance. I played ice hockey for fifteen years and regularly ponder what made certain teams work. How permaculture is a design system built on low time preference. I read, I think, occasionally I write.

There are topics I feel responsible for. Monetary systems is one.

Every election in every western country produces the same conversation about purchasing power. Cost of living. Inflation. The candidates offer explanations: supply chains, corporate greed, energy prices. Every one of them is a symptom.

Nobody names the cause. For seventy years, every government in the developed world has run the same money printer. The cost has been distributed silently across savings accounts, wages, and time. It is the most successful political sleight of hand of the past century.

The people who understand this see the whole conversation differently. They are not smarter. They have a different map.

That is the gap I feel responsible for. Not because anyone assigned it to me. Because I have been in rooms where the map existed and rooms where it didn't. I have seen what the difference costs.

Interest tells you what you enjoy thinking about. Responsibility tells you what you will still be writing about in ten years.
